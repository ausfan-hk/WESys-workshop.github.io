About WESys 2025

Weather is an important factor on energy generation (e.g., Photovoltaic systems, Water energy systems) and on energy demands (e.g., Low-altitude UAVs, human activities and EVs). In recent years, we see two important trends:

(1) New technology readiness by the weather foundation models (WFMs), e.g., FourCastNet (NVIDIA); Pangu (Huawei); GraphCast (Google); and Aurora (Microsoft). WFMs are neural network models, making it easy to transfer weather knowledge to energy system control and optimization;

(2) New application demands: For example, low-altitude UAVs are heavily influenced by wind (30%-40%) and temperature (20%) in their energy consumption. They will dock in buildings for charging and they will bring about non-trivial energy loads to buildings. This workshop aims to bring together interdisciplinary scholars from computer science and AI, energy systems, and climate and weather to talk about the opportunities brought about by weather and energy systems in the AI era.

Call for Submission

We invite researchers, practitioners, and students to submit abstracts for posters and discussion terms showcasing meaningful work at the intersection of AI, weather modeling, and energy systems. This is an excellent opportunity to share your cutting-edge research, ongoing projects, or preliminary results with a highly specialized audience.

Topics of Interest include,but are not limited to :

  • Integration of weather, energy, and AI-based systems
  • AI-powered weather understanding and modeling
  • AI-assisted weather data reanalysis
  • Weather-enhanced Energy Optimization
  • Deployment of integrated weather systems
  • Methodologies for the measurement and quantification of AI-powered weather systems

Submission Guidelines

The workshop solicits two types of submissions:

  • Posters (2 pages in the standard ACM conference PDF template, including references) are encouraged to:(1) Support younger researchers entering this field to share their ongoing work, and (2) Attract interdisciplinary researchers to share their practice and experience.
  • Discussion terms (no page limits) are encouraged to provide any abstract, research topic, and trends that can catalyze discussion among the workshop attendees and readers.
